Hi my name is Casie Lane and I have some advice for you!

Get Rid of Distractions!

Facebook, Twitter, your cranky aunt who just happens to drop by to tell you all about her gout will slow your roll.

Give yourself the power to say "No" to visitors and distractions in your life so you can really focus and get shit done.

Gain Context

It's easy to jump in head first into a programming language but if you have no idea what or why it is useful you will have a hard time wrapping your head around these new concepts.

    Here's what I suggest:

  • Block out time everyday to watch a few YouTube videos on the language. It doesn't matter if you don't completely understand what they are saying but it's just to familiarize yourself with the words they use.
  • Read blogs. Same as above, you may not understand but we are great at picking up patterns. You may find yourself saying, "Hey, yah. I've seen this before."
  • Read Job Descriptions. These can be a great way to help plan out your learning. From one language you'll most likely move on to others. So reading job descriptions can help give you a roadmap for future learning and will help kill overwhelm if you are planning on creating a career out of programming.
  • Join local meetups. There are others just like you going through the learning process at times an online support system just isn't the same as a live human.

Now go ahead, you can do this!
